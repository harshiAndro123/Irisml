    Did you know The three upright petals on an iris flower stand for faith, wisdom, and valor!

    Iris is a flowering plant genus of 310 accepted species with showy flowers. As well as being the scientific name, iris is also widely used as a common name for all Iris species, as well as some belonging to other closely related genera. A common name for some species is flags, while the plants of the subgenus Scorpiris are widely known as junos, particularly in horticulture. It is a popular garden flower.

    The often-segregated, monotypic genera Belamcanda (blackberry lily, I. domestica), Hermodactylus (snake's head iris, I. tuberosa), and Pardanthopsis (vesper iris, I. dichotoma) are currently included in Iris.

    • Family Name: Iridaceae.
    • Synonyms: Belamcanda, Juno, Junopsis
    • Height: 6 inch to 4 feet tall
    • Distribution Range: Asia,Europe and North America
    • Flower Colors: Different shades of purple, blue, white and yellow
    • Environmental Impact: Positive
    • Maintenance: Low
    • Blooming time: Late spring and summer

    The flower meaning is eloquence, which is associated with the message of the Greek goddess. Further, the association of irises and the afterlife is emphasised in Greek culture, as the goddess escorts the deceased from earth to heaven, guiding them to their final destination. Hence, irises were planted on the graves of loved ones.

    Why to Classify?

    The Iris flower dataset is significant because it serves as a foundational example in machine learning.

    01

    Simplicity

    The dataset is small and well-defined, with only three classes and four easily measurable features (sepal and petal length/width). This makes it a good starting point for beginners to learn about classification algorithms.

    02

    Effectiveness

    Despite its simplicity, the dataset demonstrates the power of machine learning. Algorithms can learn the subtle differences between the iris flower measurements and achieve high accuracy in classifying them.

    03

    Versatility

    The concepts learned from classifying these irises can be applied to more complex datasets with many features and classes. It's a stepping stone to tackling real-world classification problems in various fields.

    Iris Setosa

    This iris species is distinguished by its sepal and petal measurements. Iris setosa generally has the smallest flowers among the three, with sepals measuring up to 4.5 cm and petals up to 1.5 cm. Their colors can range from pale lilac to deep blue.

    Iris Versicolor

    This iris species has larger flowers compared to Iris setosa. Sepal length can reach up to 7 cm, while petal length can reach up to 5 cm. Their colors are quite variable, including blue, violet, and purple tones.

    Iris Virginica

    This iris species often has the largest flowers among the three. Sepal length can reach up to 7 cm and petal length can be up to 6 cm. Their colors are similar to Iris versicolor, with shades of blue, violet, and purple.
